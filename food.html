Pozole Rojo (Pork and Hominy Soup)

For the soup:

  • 4 quarts of water
  • 3 pounds cubed pork shoulder
  • 1 large onion
  • 1 large garlic head
  • Salt for flavor
  • 2 tablespoons of chicken bullion
  • 1 large can of white hominy, drained and rinsed

For the red sauce:

  • 10 guajillo peppers, cleaned, seeded, opened flat, and deveined
  • 2 ancho peppers, cleaned, seeded, opened flat, and deveined
  • 3 chile de arbol, cleaned, seeded, opened flat, and deveined (optional)
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 onion
  • 1 teaspoon dry Mexican oregano
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable or canola oil
  • Salt for flavor

For the garnish (optional):

  • 1 head of cabbage, finely shredded
  • 1 ½ cups onions, finely chopped
  • 1 ½ cups radishes, sliced
  • Mexican oregano
  • Limes, cut into wedges
  • Avocado, chopped
INSTRUCTIONS:

Heat water in a large stockpot. Add pork meat, onion, garlic, salt and chicken bullion. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and let simmer, partially covered for 2 and half hours or until meat is tender. While cooking, skim the top layer of foam and fat from the pot using a strainer. If necessary, add warm water to maintain the same level of broth in the pot. Remove pork from broth; reserve broth, onion, and garlic from the broth. Trim excess fat, and remove meat from bones. Shred meat, and cover.

Now for the sauce, soak the ancho, guajillo peppers and chile de arbol in water just enough to cover for 25-30 minutes until soft. Using a blender or food processor blend peppers, garlic cloves(from broth), onion(from broth), and oregano, adding some of the water in which they were soaking. Puree mixture until smooth.

Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high. Add the dry pepper puree and salt for flavor, stirring constantly as it splatters. Reduce heat to medium; simmer, about 25 minutes. Using a strainer, add the sauce to the broth. Bring to a boil and add the meat, and simmer gently, for about 10 minutes. Stir in white hominy, and season with salt and pepper. Simmer until heated through. Add more salt, chicken bullion or oregano if necessary.

Serve Pozole in large Mexican soup bowls and add garnishes.

NOTES:

Chile de arbol is spicy, and a little goes a long way. You can adjust the spice level by adding as much or as little as you prefer. If you're sensitive to heat, start with a small amount and taste as you go.

If you want to use uncook cacahuazintle corn (hominy) and cook it yourself, rinse the corn, remove any grain that looks dry or old. Place the corn in a pot with enough water to cover it at least 8-10 centimeters above the corn kernels. Add a quarter of a white onion, two cloves of garlic, you can also herbs like bay leaves and thyme. Cook for about an hour and a half until the corn opens. It may take less or longer, it depends on how old the corn is. This is for 1-½ lb. of corn. When the corn is already cooked, drain it and add it to the pozole as indicated in the recipe.

You can prepare the sauce a couple of days in advance so that it is not so elaborate to do it all the same day. The sauce can also be frozen and used for other stews such as adobos or enchiladas.

The Pozole tends to enhance its flavors the next day, so if you are going to prepare it for a party or special occasion you can cook it in advance, and just reheat it. Besides, it keeps very well in the freezer including the hominy, and it takes great when you reheat it weeks later.
